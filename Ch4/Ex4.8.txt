Question: Explain when operands are evaluated in the logical AND, logical OR, and equality operators.

Answer: 

Logical AND operator: the left operand is always evaluated, the right operand is only evaluated if the left operand is true.

Logical OR operator: the left operand is always evaluated, the right operand is only evaluated if the left operand is false.

Equality operators: both operands of the equality operators are always evaluated.
